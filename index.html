Friends of Dharmic Life

Founded by individuals in the Georgetown University community, Friends of Dharmic Life is a youth-led initiative that seeks to establish sustained funding for Dharmic organizations, chaplaincies and student activities at higher education institutions in the United States. This organization understands “Dharma” to encapsulate a multitude of spiritual traditions including but not limited to theist (Śaiva, Śākta, Vaiṣṇava, Smārta, Sikh, Sanātana/Hindu) and non-theist (Buddha, Jain, Yoga) traditions.

We aim to:

  • connect students, alumni, and organizations at local colleges and universities to Dharmic centers, institutions, and resources in their region;
  • provide templates and support fundraising activities; and
  • form a network of Dharmic students and educators at higher education institutions across the United States.

What is Dharmic Life at Georgetown?

Dharmic Life at Georgetown seeks to equip every seeker with relevant, logical, and well-explained teachings and practices from the diverse depths of the Dharmas.

We aspire to be a pluralistic space that welcomes everyone inclusive of all varieties of religious/spiritual, gender, sexuality, physical ability, race, ethnicity, color, class, or caste identities.
